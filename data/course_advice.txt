Post 1
Title: Advice for choosing intro programming classes
Student Year: Sophomore
Course Mentioned: Intro to Programming
Text:
Students who are new to coding should not skip the weekly practice problems. The class feels manageable if you start assignments early, but it becomes stressful when students wait until the night before. Several students said the professor explains the basics clearly but expects students to debug independently before asking for help.

Post 2
Title: What helped me pass Data Structures
Student Year: Junior
Course Mentioned: Data Structures
Text:
The most useful habit was drawing the data structures by hand before writing code. Exams focused more on tracing algorithms and explaining time complexity than memorizing definitions. Students recommended reviewing linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, recursion, and Big-O notation every week.

Post 3
Title: Linear Algebra study tips
Student Year: Sophomore
Course Mentioned: Linear Algebra
Text:
Students said the class becomes easier if you understand matrix operations early. The professor gives examples in class that are similar in style to homework, but exams require students to explain their reasoning. Office hours are helpful, especially before quizzes.

Post 4
Title: Writing-heavy general education class
Student Year: First-year
Course Mentioned: College Writing
Text:
This class is not difficult if students keep up with drafts. The professor gives detailed feedback, but students need to revise carefully instead of only fixing grammar. Peer review days are useful because they show what other students are doing well.

---

Post 5
Title: Best way to balance CS and math classes
Student Year: Senior
Course Mentioned: Computer Science and Math
Text:
Students warned against taking too many project-heavy courses at the same time. A good schedule pairs one coding-heavy course with one proof or math-heavy course. Students also said that attending lab sessions early in the semester makes final projects much less stressful.
