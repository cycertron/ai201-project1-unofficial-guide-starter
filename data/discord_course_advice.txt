Message 1
Channel: #course-advice
Student Role: Senior CS major
Topic: Choosing a programming professor
Text:
For beginner programming, choose a professor who gives lots of examples in class. Some professors are very project-based, which is good if you already know the basics, but harder if this is your first coding course. Ask whether the professor allows resubmissions or gives feedback before the final deadline.

---

Message 2
Channel: #course-advice
Student Role: Junior
Topic: Exams in CS courses
Text:
Most CS exams are not about copying code from memory. They usually ask you to trace code, explain output, identify bugs, or compare algorithms. If you can explain your homework solutions out loud, you are usually prepared for the exam.

Message 3
Channel: #math-help
Student Role: Sophomore
Topic: Linear Algebra professor expectations
Text:
The professor cares about showing steps. Even when the final answer is correct, students lose points if the reasoning is unclear. For quizzes, review class examples and make sure you understand why each row operation is valid.

---

Message 4
Channel: #first-year-advice
Student Role: Resident assistant
Topic: Office hours
Text:
Students often wait too long to go to office hours. Professors are more helpful when you bring a specific question or a piece of code that is not working. Going before the first exam helps because the professor learns your name and can explain what to focus on.

---

Message 5
Channel: #registration
Student Role: Senior
Topic: Building a balanced schedule
Text:
Avoid taking three courses with major final projects in the same semester. Ask older students which classes have weekly homework, which have big exams, and which have semester-long projects. A balanced schedule usually has a mix of exam-based, writing-based, and project-based classes.
